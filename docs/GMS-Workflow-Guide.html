1. Overview & Role Legend

This document maps every major workflow in the Grant Management System, showing how each role participates, how workflows connect to each other, and how the platform keeps all data, documents, and communications centralized — eliminating scattered spreadsheets, emails, and manual tracking.

Superadmin Platform administrator
Manager Grant manager / engagement lead
Staff Accountant / data entry
Client Client organization contact
Core Principle: Every workflow feeds into the platform's centralized audit trail. No action exists in isolation — every data entry, approval, communication, and document upload is logged, timestamped, attributed to a user, and tied to the relevant grant and organization. This creates a single source of truth that replaces email chains, scattered spreadsheets, and manual tracking.

2. Platform Onboarding & Setup Workflow

New Client Organization Setup

  1. Superadmin Creates the new client organization in the system (name, fiscal year start, contact details, approved network addresses)
  2. Superadmin Assigns staff members and managers to the organization
  3. Superadmin Creates client user accounts with appropriate permission tiers (Summary Only / Standard / Full Detail)
  4. Manager Sets up organization-specific settings: indirect cost rates, fringe benefit rates, employee positions
  5. Manager Creates grants for the organization (grant details, funder info, budget, billing model)
  6. Client Receives invitation email → logs in → completes the onboarding wizard → gains portal access

3. Grant Setup & Lifecycle Workflow

Grant Creation to Closure

  1. Manager Creates grant record: grant name, number, funder details, type (restricted/reimbursable), billing model, start/end dates, opening balance
  2. Manager Sets compliance requirements: matching %, indirect cost %, single audit threshold, CFDA number
  3. Staff / Manager Enters budget details (three-layer: total → category lines → periods)
  4. Manager Transitions lifecycle status as the grant progresses
  5. Manager Initiates closeout when grant period ends
  6. Manager Marks grant as "Closed" after all reconciliation is complete

4. Budget & Workbook Workflow

Budget Setup and Workbook Lifecycle

  1. Manager Creates budget with total award amount
  2. Staff / Manager Adds budget lines by category (personnel, supplies, travel, contractual, etc.)
  3. Staff / Manager Allocates budget across fiscal periods
  4. System automatically creates fiscal-year workbooks when data entry begins
  5. Staff Enters monthly expenses, adjustments, and journal entries into the open workbook
  6. Manager Reviews entries and submits workbook for review ("Under Review" status)
  7. Superadmin / Manager Locks or approves the workbook

5. Expense Tracking & Approval Workflow

Expense Entry Flow

  1. Staff Enters expenses by month, category, vendor, and invoice number into the open workbook
  2. System validates expense against budget category and flags any overspend
  3. Staff / Manager Flags disallowed expenses (excluded from balance calculations)
  4. Manager Reviews entries, makes adjustments or journal entries as needed
  5. Client (if permitted) Views expense summaries in the client portal

Client Expense Submission Flow (Reimbursable Grants)

  1. Client Submits reimbursable expense with attached receipts/invoices via the client portal
  2. Staff / Manager Reviews submission, verifies documentation
  3. Staff Records approved expense in the grant workbook
  4. System updates budget balances and financial reports automatically

6. Drawdown & Cash Receipt Workflow

Requesting & Receiving Funds

  1. Staff / Manager Creates a drawdown request specifying the period and amount
  2. Manager Reviews and approves the drawdown (approved amount may differ from requested)
  3. Manager Submits to funder (tracked in system)
  4. Staff Records cash receipt when funds arrive (date, amount, reference)
  5. System reconciles: drawdown requested vs. approved vs. received

7. Reimbursable Grant Billing Workflow

Hourly Rate Billing Model

  1. Manager Sets up employee positions, hourly rates, and effort allocations for the grant
  2. Staff Enters employee hours worked per period
  3. System calculates billable amount (Hours × Rate) plus indirect costs if applicable
  4. Manager Reviews and generates invoice
  5. Manager Sends invoice to funder
  6. Staff Records payment when received

Expense + Indirect Cost Billing Model

  1. Staff Enters direct expenses in the workbook
  2. System applies the grant's indirect cost rate automatically
  3. Manager Generates invoice (direct expenses + indirect cost markup)
  4. Staff Tracks payment receipt and AR aging

8. Compliance & Reporting Workflow

Compliance Calendar Management

  1. Manager Sets up compliance requirements per grant (type, due dates, frequency)
  2. System sends automated email reminders (7 days and 1 day before deadline)
  3. Staff Prepares the required report or submission
  4. Staff Uploads supporting documents and marks the submission as "Submitted"
  5. Manager Reviews and verifies the submission
  6. Client (if permitted) Views compliance status and upcoming deadlines in the portal
  7. Manager Updates status when funder accepts the submission

9. Single Audit & SEFA Workflow

Single Audit Management

  1. Manager Creates the single audit record for the fiscal year (auditor info, audit type)
  2. Staff Enters SEFA line items (program name, CFDA number, federal expenditures)
  3. System calculates cumulative SEFA totals across all grants
  4. Manager Reviews SEFA for accuracy against $750,000 threshold
  5. Manager Exports SEFA for auditor delivery
  6. If findings are issued, they flow into the Audit Findings Tracker (see next section)
  7. Client (if permitted) Views audit details and SEFA in the client portal

10. Audit Findings Workflow

Finding Discovery to Resolution

  1. Manager Logs the audit finding (finding number, category, severity, description, root cause)
  2. Manager Sets a response due date
  3. System notifies relevant staff and tracks the deadline
  4. Staff / Manager Prepares the corrective action response (actions, implementation timeline)
  5. Manager Submits the response
  6. Manager Updates status when the auditor accepts the response ("Resolved")
  7. Manager Marks as "Closed" when follow-up audit confirms implementation

11. Pipeline & Business Development Workflow

Grant Opportunity Tracking

  1. Manager Identifies a new grant opportunity and enters it as a "Prospect" in the pipeline
  2. Manager Qualifies the opportunity (reviews requirements, assesses fit, assigns probability score)
  3. Staff / Manager Prepares and submits the proposal (status: "Proposed")
  4. Manager Updates to "Awarded" when funding decision is positive
  5. Manager Converts pipeline opportunity to an active grant record (status: "Active")

Document Upload, Sharing & Access

  1. Staff / Manager Uploads document (award letter, amendment, compliance report, etc.) and assigns a category
  2. Manager Decides whether to share the document with the client portal
  3. Client (if permitted) Views shared documents or uploads their own (receipts, supporting docs)
  4. System logs every upload, download, and access event with user, network location, and timestamp
  5. Documents are organized by category and searchable via global search

14. Grant Amendment Workflow

Recording Grant Changes

  1. Manager Receives amendment notification from funder (budget change, extension, scope change, etc.)
  2. Manager Creates amendment record: type, effective date, funder reference number
  3. System automatically captures a complete snapshot of the current data before any changes are made
  4. Manager Updates grant details (new amounts, dates, etc.)
  5. System captures a snapshot of the updated data after changes are applied
  6. Manager Uploads funder's amendment documentation (if available)
  7. Complete before/after record is preserved permanently in the amendment history

15. Fiscal Year Rollover Workflow

Year-End to New Year Transition

  1. Manager Ensures all current-year workbooks are reviewed and locked/approved
  2. Manager / Superadmin Initiates fiscal year rollover for selected organizations
  3. System creates new fiscal-year workbooks for continuing grants
  4. System calculates and carries forward unspent balances from prior year
  5. Manager Optionally archives prior-year workbooks
  6. Staff Begins data entry in the new fiscal year workbooks

16. User Management & Access Control Workflow

User Lifecycle

  1. Superadmin Creates user account (name, email, role, organization assignment)
  2. System sends invitation email with temporary password
  3. User logs in and is prompted to change password (meets password policy requirements)
  4. Superadmin Adjusts organization assignments as needed (takes effect within 5 minutes)
  5. Superadmin Can force password reset at any time
  6. Superadmin Deactivates user when they leave (reason logged, deactivation timestamped)
  7. Superadmin Can force-logout any active session immediately

17. Security Monitoring Workflow

Ongoing Security Operations

  1. Superadmin Reviews login history for anomalies (unusual locations, failed login spikes, off-hours access)
  2. Superadmin Monitors active sessions (who is logged in, from where, for how long)
  3. Superadmin Reviews audit logs for unauthorized or suspicious actions
  4. Superadmin Exports audit logs for SOC 2 evidence packages
  5. Superadmin Configures approved network address lists per organization (restricts access to authorized office networks only)
  6. Superadmin Manages two-factor authentication requirements and password security settings
  7. Superadmin Force-logouts compromised sessions if detected
Time saved: Security monitoring is built into the platform — no separate security monitoring or log management tool needed. All evidence for SOC 2 or regulatory reviews is exportable directly from the system.

Report Generation

  4. Manager Exports to Excel or PDF
  5. System logs the export event (who exported what, when, and from which location)
  6. Client (if permitted) Generates their own financial summaries from the client portal

20. How GMS Eliminates Scattered Communications

Before GMS (Scattered)
  • Grant data in multiple Excel spreadsheets (one per grant, sometimes per year)
  • Client communications via email (compliance requests, document requests, status updates)
  • Documents stored in shared drives, email attachments, and local folders
  • Compliance deadlines tracked in Outlook calendars or separate spreadsheets
  • Amendment history reconstructed from email chains
  • Audit findings tracked in Word documents or email threads
  • No centralized security monitoring
  • Manual fiscal year transition (copy spreadsheets, recalculate balances)
After GMS (Centralized)
  • Centralized database with real-time calculations and budget health indicators
  • Built-in messaging system with threaded conversations and file attachments
  • Centralized document management with category organization and access logging
  • Compliance calendar with automated email reminders and submission tracking
  • Amendment history with before/after snapshots and attached documentation
  • Audit findings tracker with structured response workflow and status progression
  • Complete audit trail, login history, session monitoring, and exportable SOC 2 evidence
  • Automated fiscal year rollover with carryforward calculations
